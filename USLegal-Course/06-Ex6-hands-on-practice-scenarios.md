# Exercise 6: Hands-On Practice — Real Scenarios (30 min)
---

This is your time to put everything together. Choose **two scenarios** from the options below — one from Existing Business and one from Net New Business. Each scenario spans multiple apps, just like your real workflow. Work through them at your own pace, and your trainer is available for questions.

> [!TIP]
> For every scenario, think about the **HubSpot bridge**: What would you pull from HubSpot before starting? What would you log back into HubSpot after?

---

## Existing Business Scenarios

### Scenario A: The Quiet Account Revival

**Situation:** Morrison & Associates, a 70-attorney firm, has been a US Legal client for 2 years using document retrieval and court filing services. Six months ago, their primary contact (the Office Manager) left the firm. Since then, usage has dropped 60% and no one has responded to your last two emails.

**Your mission (pick at least 2):**

1. **Excel:** Open USLegal_Account_List.xlsx. Find Morrison & Associates and analyze their usage decline. Ask Copilot what patterns it sees in accounts with similar drop-offs — are there common factors? What's the typical save rate?

2. **Teams:** Using Copilot in Teams, prepare for a "re-introduction" call. You don't know who the new point of contact is. Draft a prep sheet that includes: questions to identify the new decision-maker, talking points for re-establishing the relationship, and a brief value recap of what US Legal has delivered over 2 years.

3. **Outlook:** Draft a re-engagement email to the firm's general administrative contact. The email should acknowledge the transition, express interest in connecting with whoever now oversees legal services vendors, and offer a brief "state of your account" review as the reason to meet.

4. **Word:** If the re-engagement works and you get a meeting, create a one-page "Account Partnership Summary" for Morrison & Associates showing: services used, volume delivered, and value highlights over the relationship. This is your leave-behind.

---

### Scenario B: The Expansion Play

**Situation:** Calloway Legal Group is your strongest account — a 50-attorney firm that uses the full service suite and always has a high engagement score. Their contract renewal is next quarter. You want to use the QBR to propose a multi-year renewal with expanded scope.

**Your mission (pick at least 2):**

1. **Excel:** Open USLegal_Account_List.xlsx. Analyze Calloway's contract value relative to other firms of similar size. Ask Copilot: "Is Calloway's contract value in line with comparable accounts, or is there room to grow?" Also check what services similar-sized firms use that Calloway doesn't.

2. **Teams:** Prepare a QBR agenda using Copilot. Attach the account history document and ask Copilot to create: a relationship summary, three discussion topics backed by data, a proposed expansion recommendation, and a suggested agenda with time allocations.

3. **Word:** Create an expansion justification document proposing a 3-year renewal with an added service tier. Include a business case with projected savings from bundling services.

4. **Outlook:** Draft a QBR invitation email to Calloway's Managing Partner. Frame it as a "partnership review" — not a sales meeting. Reference specific accomplishments and hint that you have ideas for making the next year even better.

## Net New Business Scenarios

### Scenario C: The Warm Referral

**Situation:** A partner at Henderson & Blake (your existing client) mentioned that their colleague, the Managing Partner at Archer & Dunn (a 90-attorney insurance defense firm), is unhappy with their current legal services provider. This is a warm referral — your best kind of lead.

**Your mission (pick at least 2):**

1. **Teams:** Using Copilot, research what you should know about insurance defense firms as a market segment. What are their specific legal services needs? What pain points are common? Prepare a meeting prep sheet tailored to this practice area.

2. **Outlook:** Draft an outreach email that leverages the referral. Reference the Henderson & Blake connection by name (with permission). Position the email as "a colleague suggested we connect" — not a cold pitch. Make it easy to say yes to a call.

3. **Excel:** Open USLegal_Competitor_Pricing.xlsx. Analyze competitor pricing for the service categories most relevant to insurance defense firms (high-volume court filings, document retrieval for litigation). Identify where US Legal has a pricing or service advantage.

4. **Word:** Create a brief (half-page) "Why US Legal for Insurance Defense Firms" positioning document that you could attach to your outreach email or bring to the first meeting.

---

### Scenario D: The Competitive Displacement

**Situation:** Bartlett, Griggs & Chen, a 150-attorney multi-practice firm, is currently served by your biggest competitor, National Legal Services Corp. You learned through a mutual connection that they're frustrated with response times and considering alternatives. The firm uses document retrieval, court filing, and process service — the same services US Legal offers.

**Your mission (pick at least 2):**

1. **Excel:** Analyze the competitor pricing data for National Legal Services Corp specifically. Ask Copilot to compare their pricing and service model against US Legal's across the three service categories this firm uses. Identify your strongest competitive angle.

2. **Word:** Using the battle card you created in Exercise 5 (or create a new one), prepare a tailored competitive comparison that addresses this firm's specific frustration: response times. Add a section on US Legal's SLA commitments and turnaround time guarantees.

3. **Outlook:** Draft a prospecting email that subtly addresses their pain point (slow response times) without directly naming the competitor. Position US Legal's turnaround time and service quality as differentiators. Include a specific, measurable claim (e.g., "average 4-hour turnaround on standard document retrieval requests").

4. **Teams:** Prepare for a first meeting with this firm. Given that this is a competitive displacement, your prep sheet should include: questions that surface dissatisfaction with their current provider (without badmouthing), talking points about US Legal's transition support program, and objection responses for "we don't have time to switch."
