# Exercise 4: Teams — Meeting Prep & Summaries (30 min)
---

In this exercise, you use Copilot in Teams to prepare for two types of meetings you have every week: **prospect calls** (net new business) and **client reviews** (existing business). You'll also practice using Copilot to summarize meetings so you can quickly log key points into HubSpot.

Teams is where much of your real-time collaboration happens. Copilot in Teams can help you walk into every meeting prepared and walk out with a clean summary — no more scrambling to reconstruct what was said.

---

### Task 1: Prepare for a first meeting with a prospect (12 min)

You have a first call scheduled with the Managing Partner at Whitfield, Crane & Davis (the firm you researched in Exercise 1). You need to prepare questions, talking points, and a quick reference sheet.

1. In **Teams for the web**, select the **Copilot** icon in the navigation pane.

2. Ensure the **Work** toggle is selected. Attach the **USLegal_Company_Overview.docx** and **USLegal_Service_Catalog.docx** files from your OneDrive.

3. Enter the following prompt:

    **I have a first meeting with the Managing Partner at Whitfield, Crane & Davis, a 120-attorney full-service law firm with offices in Atlanta, Charlotte, and Nashville. Using the attached US Legal company overview and service catalog, help me prepare for this call. Create: (1) A brief one-paragraph summary of US Legal's most relevant services for a multi-state full-service firm, (2) Five discovery questions I should ask to understand their current legal services setup and pain points, (3) Three talking points that position US Legal's strengths for this type of firm, and (4) Two potential objections they might raise and suggested responses. Format this as a meeting prep sheet I can reference during the call.**

4. Review the meeting prep sheet. Ask Copilot to refine a specific section:

    **The discovery questions are too generic. Rewrite them to be specific to a multi-state firm. For example, instead of "What are your current pain points?" ask something that shows I understand the complexity of managing legal services across multiple jurisdictions.**

5. Review the improved questions. Ask one more refinement:

    **Add a section at the bottom with 2-3 "if they say X, respond with Y" scenarios specific to common objections in our market — like "we're happy with our current provider" or "your services are commoditized, why switch?"**

6. Copy the final meeting prep output. You'll reference this during your call.

> [!NOTE]
> **HubSpot Bridge — Before a Prospect Call:**
> 1. Pull any existing notes on the prospect from HubSpot (deal record, previous contact attempts, source).
> 2. Use Copilot in Teams to generate your prep sheet (as you just did).
> 3. Paste key talking points into the HubSpot deal record's "Meeting Prep" notes field so other team members have visibility.

---

### Task 2: Prepare for a Quarterly Business Review with an existing client (8 min)

You have a QBR coming up with Calloway Legal Group, a long-standing client. You need to summarize the relationship and prepare discussion points.

1. In **Teams Copilot**, attach the **USLegal_Client_Account_History.docx** file from your OneDrive.

2. Write your own prompt that asks Copilot to:
    - Summarize the key highlights of the Calloway Legal Group account based on the attached document
    - Prepare 3-4 discussion points for a quarterly business review, focusing on: recent service usage trends, areas where usage has increased or decreased, and potential expansion opportunities
    - Draft a "partnership value summary" — 2-3 sentences you could say to open the QBR that reinforces the value of the relationship
    - Suggest one "challenge topic" to address proactively (e.g., a service area where satisfaction might be slipping)

3. Review the output. If the partnership value summary sounds too generic, ask Copilot to make it more specific by referencing concrete results from the account history (e.g., number of filings completed, turnaround time improvements).

4. Copy the QBR prep output.

> [!NOTE]
> **HubSpot Bridge — Before a Client Review:**
> Pull the client's full activity history from HubSpot, including recent support tickets, billing changes, and any notes from other team members. Include these details in your Copilot prompt for more personalized prep.

---

### Task 3: Summarize a meeting for HubSpot logging (10 min)

After meetings, you need to quickly log key points, decisions, and next steps into HubSpot. Copilot can turn a meeting transcript into a clean, structured summary you can paste directly into your CRM.

**Part A: Summarize a prospect meeting**

1. In **Teams Copilot**, attach the **USLegal_Prospect_Meeting_Transcript.docx** file from your OneDrive.

2. Enter:

    **Analyze this meeting transcript from a first call with a new prospect. Extract: (1) Key pain points the prospect mentioned, (2) Services they expressed interest in, (3) Objections or concerns they raised, (4) Agreed-upon next steps with owners and dates, and (5) A 2-3 sentence deal summary I can paste into HubSpot. Format this as a structured CRM note — use short labels and bullet points.**

3. Review the summary. This format should be ready to copy-paste directly into HubSpot's activity log.

**Part B: Summarize a client review meeting**

1. Attach the **USLegal_Client_QBR_Meeting_Transcript.docx** file.

2. Write your own prompt to summarize this existing client meeting. Your prompt should ask Copilot to extract:
    - Client satisfaction signals (positive and negative)
    - Expansion opportunities discussed
    - Action items with owners
    - Any risk signals (mentions of competitors, budget concerns, dissatisfaction)
    - A brief summary suitable for HubSpot

3. Review and compare to the prospect summary format. Both should be CRM-ready.

> [!NOTE]
> **HubSpot Bridge — After Any Call:**
> 1. Copilot summarizes the meeting in Teams (as you just practiced).
> 2. Copy the structured summary.
> 3. In HubSpot, navigate to the relevant contact/deal record and log the call activity.
> 4. Paste the summary into the notes field.
> 5. Update the deal stage if needed and create follow-up tasks based on the action items Copilot extracted.

> [!TIP]
> **Pro tip for live meetings:** If you're in a Teams meeting with transcription enabled, Copilot can summarize the meeting in real-time. After the meeting ends, open the meeting chat, select the **Copilot** icon, and ask it to summarize the discussion, list action items, or identify key decisions. This works automatically — no need to upload a transcript.
