# Exercise 5: Word — Competitive Positioning & Sales Tools (20 min)
---

In this exercise, you use Copilot in Word to create the documents your sales team keeps open alongside their tools every day: battle cards for competitive situations, objection-handling guides, and expansion justification summaries. These are the deliverables that make the difference when a prospect says "your competitor is cheaper" or when you need to justify adding a new service line to an existing account.

---

### Task 1: Create a competitive battle card (12 min)

A battle card is a quick-reference document your team uses during sales conversations. It summarizes how US Legal stacks up against a specific competitor and provides ready-made responses to common objections.

1. In **Word for the web**, create a blank document.

2. In the **Draft with Copilot** prompt field that appears above the document, enter the following prompt. Attach the **USLegal_Company_Overview.docx** and **USLegal_Service_Catalog.docx** files.

    **Create a competitive battle card for US Legal's sales team. This battle card compares US Legal against our primary competitor, National Legal Services Corp. Use the attached company overview and service catalog as the source for US Legal's strengths. Structure the battle card with these sections:**

    **1. Quick Comparison Table — Side-by-side comparison across key dimensions: Service breadth, Pricing model, Turnaround time, Technology/portal capabilities, Client support model, Geographic coverage.**

    **2. Where We Win — 3-4 bullet points on US Legal's clear advantages (service quality, relationships, flexibility, regional expertise).**

    **3. Where They Compete — Honest assessment of 2-3 areas where the competitor is strong (don't hide weaknesses — reps need to know).**

    **4. Objection Handlers — For each of these common objections, provide a 2-3 sentence response:**
    - **"We're happy with our current provider."**
    - **"Your competitor is cheaper."**
    - **"Your services are commoditized — why should we switch?"**
    - **"We don't have time to transition right now."**

    **5. Killer Questions — 3 discovery questions that expose weaknesses in the competitor's offering without directly attacking them.**

    **Keep the tone confident and factual. This is for internal use — reps need honest, actionable intelligence, not marketing fluff. Keep the entire document to 2 pages maximum.**

3. Review draft 1. The battle card should be practical and honest. If the objection handlers sound too generic or scripted, refine them:

    **Rewrite the objection handlers to sound more conversational — like what a rep would actually say on a call, not what a marketing team would write. For the "your competitor is cheaper" objection, include the talking points about total cost of ownership and service bundling advantages.**

    > [!TIP]
    > If you completed the Excel exercise (Exercise 3), you captured talking points about pricing advantages. Reference those here by including them in your prompt or pasting them into the document after Copilot generates it.

4. Review draft 2. If you want to keep this version, select **Keep it**. If you want one more pass, ask Copilot to add a section:

    **Add a "Competitive Landmines" section at the end — 2-3 things to avoid saying or doing when competing against this competitor (e.g., don't lead with price, don't bash their technology).**

5. Select **Keep it** on your preferred draft. Save the document as **USLegal_Battle_Card_vs_NationalLegal.docx** in your OneDrive.

### Task 2: Create an expansion justification summary (8 min)

When you want to pitch a new service to an existing client, you need a short document that makes the business case. This is a one-page summary that you can email to your contact or bring to a meeting.

1. Create a new blank document in **Word for the web**.

2. In the **Draft with Copilot** prompt field, attach the **USLegal_Service_Catalog.docx** and **USLegal_Client_Account_History.docx** files. Enter:

    **Create a one-page expansion justification summary for Henderson & Blake LLP, an existing US Legal client (85-attorney litigation firm). They currently use our document retrieval service only. I want to pitch our compliance filing service as an add-on. Using the attached service catalog and account history, create a document with:**

    **1. Current Partnership Summary — Brief recap of what Henderson & Blake uses today and the value delivered.**

    **2. Recommended Addition — Overview of the compliance filing service and why it's relevant for a litigation-heavy firm of their size.**

    **3. Business Case — 3-4 bullet points on expected benefits: time savings, error reduction, compliance risk mitigation, and cost efficiency vs. managing it in-house.**

    **4. Proposed Next Step — A specific, low-commitment ask (e.g., a 30-day pilot, a side-by-side comparison, or a brief demo).**

    **Tone: advisory and client-focused. This should feel like a trusted partner recommendation, not a sales pitch. Keep it to one page.**

3. Review the draft. If the business case bullets are too vague, ask Copilot to make them more concrete:

    **Make the business case bullets more specific. Instead of "save time," quantify it — "firms of similar size typically reduce compliance filing turnaround by 40%." Include at least one data point or benchmark per bullet, even if approximate.**

4. Select **Keep it** and save as **USLegal_Expansion_Summary_Henderson_Blake.docx**.
