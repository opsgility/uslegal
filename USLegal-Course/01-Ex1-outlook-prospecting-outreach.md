# Exercise 1: Outlook — Prospecting & Net New Outreach (40 min)
---

In this exercise, you focus on the **Net New Business** side of your dual mandate: prospecting into law firms you don't currently serve. You'll use Copilot in Outlook to draft personalized outreach that doesn't sound templated, prepare for first meetings, and write compelling follow-ups.

> [!TIP]
> Remember the four elements of an effective prompt: **Goal, Context, Sources, Expectations.** You'll use these throughout.

### Scenario

You're a Sales Representative at US Legal. Your territory includes mid-size and large law firms in the Southeast region. You've identified three target firms for this quarter:

- **Parker & Associates** — a 60-attorney litigation firm that recently expanded their practice areas
- **Whitfield, Crane & Davis** — a 120-attorney full-service firm with offices in three states
- **Meridian Law Group** — a 40-attorney boutique firm specializing in corporate law

Your goal is to use Copilot in Outlook to craft outreach that feels personal, references real firm details, and positions US Legal's value in a commoditized market.

---

### Task 1: Draft a cold prospecting email to a new law firm (15 min)

You're reaching out to the Managing Partner at Parker & Associates for the first time. You want an email that feels personal — not like a mass template.

1. In **Outlook on the web**, create a new email.

2. In the body of the message, select the **Open Copilot** (pencil) icon to open the **Draft with Copilot** window.

3. In the Copilot prompt field, enter a prompt that asks Copilot to draft a cold outreach email. Here's an example of a strong prompt:

    **I'm a Sales Representative at US Legal, a legal services provider. Draft a cold prospecting email to the Managing Partner at Parker & Associates, a 60-attorney litigation firm that recently expanded into employment law and intellectual property. Position US Legal as a partner that helps firms like theirs scale without adding overhead — particularly around document retrieval, court filing services, and compliance support. The tone should feel like a knowledgeable peer reaching out, not a vendor pitching. Keep it under 150 words and end with a soft ask for a 15-minute call.**

4. Review the draft Copilot generates. Consider:
    - Does it feel personal or templated?
    - Does it reference something specific about Parker & Associates?
    - Is the call-to-action appropriate for a cold first touch?

5. The draft is decent but could be stronger. In the Copilot prompt field below the draft, ask Copilot to refine it:

    **Make the opening line more specific — reference their recent expansion into employment law and frame it as the reason I'm reaching out. Also remove any generic phrases like "I hope this finds you well."**

6. Review draft 2 of 2. Try one more refinement — ask Copilot to adjust the tone:

    **Change the tone to be more confident and direct while still being respectful. Our market is commoditized, so I need to stand out by showing I understand their world, not just listing our services.**

7. Navigate between drafts 1, 2, and 3 using the forward and backward arrows. Select the version you prefer and click **Keep it**.

8. After the draft is inserted into the email body, review it one final time. If needed, highlight the full email, select the **Open Copilot** (pencil) icon, and use **Change Tone** to experiment with different tones (Formal, Casual, Direct, etc.).

> [!NOTE]
> **HubSpot Bridge:** After sending this email, you would log the outreach in HubSpot as a new contact activity under the Parker & Associates company record. Copy the final email text to your clipboard before sending so you can paste it into HubSpot's activity log.

### Task 2: Research a target firm and draft informed outreach (15 min)

Before reaching out to Whitfield, Crane & Davis, you want to do your homework. You'll use Copilot Chat for research, then bring those insights into an Outlook email.

1. Open a new tab and navigate to **Microsoft 365** (microsoft365.com). Select **Copilot** in the navigation pane.

2. Ensure the **Web** toggle is selected (for external information). Enter the following research prompt:

    **I'm preparing to prospect into Whitfield, Crane & Davis, a 120-attorney full-service law firm with offices in Atlanta, Charlotte, and Nashville. What should I know before my first outreach? Summarize the firm's practice areas, any recent news or leadership changes, and potential pain points a legal services provider like US Legal could address. Focus on publicly available information.**

3. Review Copilot Chat's response. Note 2-3 specific insights you can reference in your outreach email.

4. Now switch to **Outlook on the web** and create a new email. Open the **Draft with Copilot** window.

5. Write your own prompt that incorporates the research insights you just gathered. Your prompt should:
    - Reference a specific detail about Whitfield, Crane & Davis (practice areas, recent news, multi-state presence)
    - Position US Legal's value in terms relevant to that firm
    - Keep the email concise and end with a clear next step

6. Iterate on the draft at least once. Focus on making the email sound like you've done your research — not like you're reading from a script.

7. Select **Keep it** when you're satisfied.

> [!TIP]
> **Prompt quality matters.** A prompt like "Write an email to a law firm" will give you a generic result. A prompt like "Draft outreach to a 120-attorney multi-state firm whose Atlanta office just hired three lateral partners in healthcare law — position our compliance filing services as relevant to their growth" will give you something usable.

### Task 3: Draft a post-meeting follow-up (10 min)

You just had a first meeting with the Office Administrator at Meridian Law Group. The conversation went well — they expressed interest in US Legal's court filing services but had concerns about transition costs from their current provider.

1. In **Outlook on the web**, create a new email. Open the **Draft with Copilot** window.

2. Write a prompt asking Copilot to draft a follow-up email. Include these details in your prompt:
    - You met with the Office Administrator at Meridian Law Group
    - They're interested in court filing services
    - Their concern is transition costs from their current provider
    - You want to address the concern directly and propose a next step (a pilot program or a detailed cost comparison)

3. Review the draft. Ask Copilot to refine it by:

    **Add a specific line that acknowledges their concern about transition costs and offers to provide a side-by-side cost comparison showing total cost of ownership over 12 months. Keep the tone confident but not pushy.**

4. Select **Keep it** when satisfied.

> [!NOTE]
> **HubSpot Bridge:** After this meeting, you would: (1) Log meeting notes in HubSpot under the Meridian Law Group deal record, (2) Update the deal stage, (3) Set a follow-up task. Copy key points from your Copilot-drafted follow-up to paste into HubSpot's notes field.
