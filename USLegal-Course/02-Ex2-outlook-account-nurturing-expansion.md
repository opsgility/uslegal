# Exercise 2: Outlook — Account Nurturing & Expansion (30 min)
---

In this exercise, you focus on the **Existing Business** side of your dual mandate: retaining current clients, expanding spend, and reaching new contacts within firms you already serve. You'll use Copilot in Outlook to craft touchpoints that feel genuine — not like automated check-ins — and to create messaging that opens doors to new services and new buyers.

### Scenario

You manage several existing US Legal accounts. Here are three that need attention this week:

| Account | Status | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| **Henderson & Blake LLP** | Active client, 85-attorney firm. Uses document retrieval only. Last meaningful contact was 3 months ago. | Cross-sell compliance filing services; reach the new Director of Operations who joined 2 months ago. |
| **Calloway Legal Group** | Long-standing client, 50-attorney firm. Uses full service suite. Renewal conversation coming up next quarter. | Strengthen relationship; prepare for QBR. |
| **Sterling, Pratt & Nguyen** | Was an active client until 6 months ago. Stopped responding to emails. Still under contract but usage has dropped significantly. | Re-engage before renewal lapses. |

---

### Task 1: Draft a check-in email that isn't "just checking in" (10 min)

Henderson & Blake LLP has been quiet for 3 months. You need to reach out, but a generic "just checking in" email won't cut it.

1. In **Outlook on the web**, create a new email. Select the **Open Copilot** (pencil) icon to open the **Draft with Copilot** window.

2. Attach the **USLegal_Client_Account_History.docx** file from your OneDrive. This file contains recent account activity and service usage details.

3. Enter a prompt like:

    **I'm a Sales Rep at US Legal. Draft a check-in email to my main contact at Henderson & Blake LLP, an 85-attorney firm that currently uses our document retrieval service. I haven't spoken with them in 3 months. Reference the attached account history to mention something specific about their recent usage. Don't use the phrase "just checking in." Instead, lead with a relevant insight or observation — maybe a trend in their usage, an industry development, or a way we could help them be more efficient. Keep it under 120 words and suggest a brief call.**

4. Review the draft. Copilot should have referenced details from the attached account history. If the email still feels generic, refine it:

    **Make the opening more specific — reference a concrete detail from their account history, like a spike in document retrieval requests or a new practice area they've been filing in. Frame the email as proactive, not reactive.**

5. Select **Keep it** when satisfied.

---

### Task 2: Draft a warm introduction to a new contact at an existing firm (10 min)

Henderson & Blake recently hired a new Director of Operations. You want to introduce yourself and US Legal's services without being pushy — leveraging the existing relationship with the firm.

1. Create a new email in **Outlook on the web**. Select the **Open Copilot** (pencil) icon to open the **Draft with Copilot** window.

2. Write your own prompt from scratch. Your prompt should include:
    - You're reaching out to a **new contact** (Director of Operations) at a firm where US Legal **already has a relationship**
    - Reference the existing partnership — the firm has been a client for several years using document retrieval services
    - The goal is to introduce yourself, not to sell immediately
    - Mention you'd love to share how the firm currently uses US Legal and explore whether there are ways to support their operations team specifically
    - Tone: warm, collegial, brief

3. Review the draft and iterate at least once. Consider asking Copilot to:
    - Add a line congratulating them on their new role
    - Reference a specific service area that's relevant to an operations leader (e.g., compliance filings, process automation)

4. Select **Keep it** when satisfied.

> [!TIP]
> **Prompt quality tip:** Instead of "Write an email to a new contact," try: "Draft a warm introduction to someone who just joined a firm I already work with — reference our existing partnership and make it feel like an insider welcome, not a cold pitch."

---

### Task 3: Draft a cross-sell message and a re-engagement email (10 min)

You have two emails to write. For each, write your own prompt and iterate with Copilot.

**Email A: Cross-sell to Henderson & Blake**

Draft an email to your existing contact at Henderson & Blake introducing US Legal's compliance filing service. They currently only use document retrieval. Your prompt should:
- Reference the existing relationship and their current usage
- Position the new service as a natural extension, not an upsell
- Include a specific benefit (e.g., "firms that bundle retrieval and compliance filing see a 20% reduction in turnaround time")
- Tone: helpful advisor, not salesperson

**Email B: Re-engage Sterling, Pratt & Nguyen**

Draft an email to re-engage a client who has gone quiet. Their usage dropped significantly 6 months ago but they're still under contract. Your prompt should:
- Acknowledge the gap without being accusatory ("I noticed we haven't connected in a while")
- Lead with value — maybe a new feature, a recent improvement, or an industry insight
- Make it easy to re-engage (offer a quick call, a usage review, or a "here's what's new" summary)
- Tone: genuine concern, not desperation

For each email, iterate with Copilot at least once before selecting **Keep it**.

> [!NOTE]
> **HubSpot Bridge — Existing Account Workflows:**
> - **Before writing:** Pull the client's recent activity from HubSpot to inform your prompt (last contact date, deal stage, service usage).
> - **After writing:** Log the outreach in HubSpot. Update contact records if you're reaching out to a new person at the firm.
> - **For cross-sell:** Create a new deal in HubSpot linked to the existing company record with the appropriate service line.
